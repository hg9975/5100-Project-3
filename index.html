Overview of Job Opportunities in Big Data Path

Are you planning to pursue a career path in big data?

Big Data is a field that treats ways to handle, analyze, and extract important information from large, complex, hard-to-manage volumes of data that inundate business on a day-to-day basis. With the fast development of technologies, the importance of big data has been recognized and valued recent years. By analyzing the data, businesses can

  • improve operational efficiency
  • optimize product development
  • explore new revenue and growth opportunities
  • streamline resource management

As big data becomes a focus in the business world, more and more data-related job opportunities available. Business Analyst, Data Analyst, and Data Scientist, as three hot positions under the big data path, have significant amount of overlaps in their job responsibilities and required skills. For example, they all work with massive data to solve business problems. Consequently, when it comes to determine a career path, you may hesitate among these three positions. You may wonder:

  • What are differences among these three positions?
  • Which one provides more job opportunities?
  • What industries can I work in?
  • Which position is more suitable for me?

Therefore, we collected 7364 jobs in these three positions in the U.S., which include 2974 Business Analyst jobs, 1564 Data Analyst jobs, and 2826 Data Scientist jobs, and created this project. In the following visualizations, you can view and compare job distribution, salaries, and company ratings of these three positions. Hopefully, we can help you find answers to above questions.
